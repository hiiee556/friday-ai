Hey Friday, let's break down this bubble sort thing in a super simple way.

**Imagine You're Cleaning Your Room**

You've got a bunch of toys and books all over the floor, and you want to put them in order from smallest to largest. That's kinda like what bubble sort does with a list of numbers.

**Here's How You'd Do It**

1. **Start at the beginning**: You begin with the first toy or book on the floor.
2. **Compare it to the next one**: You look at the next toy or book and say, "Hey, is this one smaller or bigger than the one I'm holding?"
3. **Swap if it's smaller**: If the next one is smaller, you swap them. Now the smaller one is in your hand, and the bigger one is on the floor.
4. **Move to the next one**: You keep doing this with each toy or book, comparing and swapping if necessary.
5. **Keep going until you're done**: You keep repeating this process until you've looked at every single toy or book on the floor.

**That's Bubble Sort in a Nutshell**

It's like cleaning your room, but instead of toys and books, you're sorting numbers. And just like how you might need to make multiple passes through your room to get everything tidy, bubble sort makes multiple passes through the list to get the numbers in order.

**Example Time**

Let's say you've got a list of numbers: `[5, 2, 8, 3, 1, 6, 4]`. You'd start at the beginning, compare the first two numbers (5 and 2), and swap them because 2 is smaller. Then you'd move on to the next number, and so on.

**Code Time**

Here's some code that does the same thing:
```python
def bubble_sort(arr):
    n = len(arr)
    for i in range(n-1):
        for j in range(n-i-1):
            if arr[j] > arr[j+1]:
                arr[j], arr[j+1] = arr[j+1], arr[j]
    return arr

# Example usage:
arr = [5, 2, 8, 3, 1, 6, 4]
sorted_arr = bubble_sort(arr)
print(sorted_arr)  # [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8]
```
**That's It**

Bubble sort is a simple way to sort a list of numbers. It's not the fastest way, but it's easy to understand and can be helpful for small lists or for learning about sorting algorithms.
