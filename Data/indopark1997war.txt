**INDO-PAKISTAN WAR OF 1999**

**NOT A WAR IN 1997 BUT 1999**

The Indo-Pakistani War of 1999, also known as the Kargil War, was a military conflict between India and Pakistan that took place in the disputed region of Kashmir. The war began in May 1999 and lasted for several months.

**CAUSES OF THE WAR:**

1. **Infiltration of Pakistani troops:** In May 1999, Pakistani troops and militants infiltrated into the Indian-administered region of Kashmir, occupying strategic heights and positions.
2. **Indian response:** India responded by launching a military operation to retake the occupied positions, known as Operation Vijay.
3. **Escalation:** The conflict escalated, with both sides suffering heavy casualties and damage to infrastructure.

**KEY EVENTS OF THE WAR:**

1. **Infiltration:** On May 3, 1999, Pakistani troops and militants infiltrated into the Indian-administered region of Kashmir, occupying strategic heights and positions.
2. **Indian response:** On May 9, 1999, India launched Operation Vijay, a military operation to retake the occupied positions.
3. **Battle of Tiger Hill:** On June 26, 1999, Indian troops captured the strategic peak of Tiger Hill, a key location in the conflict.
4. **Ceasefire:** On July 26, 1999, a ceasefire was agreed upon by both sides, with the help of international mediators.

**CONSEQUENCES OF THE WAR:**

1. **Casualties:** The war resulted in heavy casualties, with estimates suggesting that over 1,000 people were killed on both sides.
2. **Infrastructure damage:** The conflict caused significant damage to infrastructure, including roads, bridges, and buildings.
3. **International involvement:** The war led to international involvement, with the United States, Russia, and other countries playing a role in mediating the conflict.
