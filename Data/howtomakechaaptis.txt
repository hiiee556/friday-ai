The classic Indian flatbread - chapaties (also known as chapatis or rotis)! Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make chapaties:

**Ingredients:**

* 2 cups of whole wheat flour (atta)
* 1/2 teaspoon of salt
* 1/4 teaspoon of sugar
* 1/2 cup of lukewarm water
* Ghee or oil for brushing

**Instructions:**

1. **Prepare the dough:**
	* In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, salt, and sugar.
	* Gradually add the lukewarm water to the flour mixture and knead the dough for 5-7 minutes.
	* The dough should be soft, smooth, and pliable.
2. **Knead the dough:**
	* Knead the dough for an additional 2-3 minutes to develop the gluten.
	* The dough should be elastic and smooth.
3. **Rest the dough:**
	* Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
	* This will help the gluten to relax, making the dough easier to roll out.
4. **Divide the dough:**
	* Divide the dough into 6-8 equal portions, depending on the size of chapaties you prefer.
	* Shape each portion into a ball.
5. **Roll out the dough:**
	* Use a rolling pin to roll out each ball of dough into a thin circle, about 6-7 inches in diameter.
	* The dough should be rolled out evenly, with no thick spots.
6. **Heat the tava or griddle:**
	* Preheat a non-stick tava or griddle over medium heat.
	* Once the tava is hot, place a rolled-out chapatie on it.
7. **Cook the chapatie:**
	* Cook the chapatie for 30-45 seconds, or until it starts to puff up and the edges start to curl.
	* Flip the chapatie over and cook for an additional 30-45 seconds, or until it's golden brown and cooked through.
8. **Brush with ghee or oil:**
	* Brush the cooked chapatie with ghee or oil to give it a soft and shiny texture.
9. **Serve:**
	* Serve the chapaties hot with your favorite curry, dal, or vegetable dish.

**Tips:**

* Use a cast-iron or non-stick tava to cook the chapaties, as they retain heat well.
* Adjust the heat as needed to prevent the chapaties from burning.
* Don't overwork the dough, as it can lead to tough chapaties.
* Use a gentle touch when rolling out the dough to avoid developing the gluten too much.

**Common mistakes to avoid:**

* Overworking the dough, which can lead to tough chapaties.
* Not resting the dough long enough, which can make it difficult to roll out.
* Not using the right type of flour, which can affect the texture and taste of the chapaties.
* Not cooking the chapaties at the right temperature, which can lead to burning or undercooking.

**Variations:**

* You can add different types of flour, such as all-purpose flour or whole wheat flour with bran, to create different textures and flavors.
* You can add spices, herbs, or other ingredients, such as cumin seeds, coriander seeds, or chopped cilantro, to create flavored chapaties.
* You can use a skillet or frying pan to cook the chapaties, instead of a tava or griddle.
